Praxis Initiative is a directly impacted-led 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to transforming the criminal legal system through independent prison oversight, overdose prevention, harm reduction, arts programming, and civics training for returning citizens.

Meet Our Team

The people powering our mission for change. Driven by experience, united by purpose.

John Fabricius

Executive Director
John endured fifteen years of incarceration spanning nine distinct prison complexes, acquiring deep institutional knowledge of security operations, administrative processes, and inmate services. He channels this lived expertise into visionary reform strategies, driving legislative advocacy efforts and empowering community-driven oversight initiatives aimed at transforming Arizona's criminal legal system from the inside out.

J Alexandria Hunt-Garcia

Policy Director & Co-founder
J served five years incarcerated over three convictions, gaining firsthand comprehension of carceral policies, systemic barriers, and rehabilitative challenges. Recognized for leadership in drug policy and substance use advocacy, she leverages her lived experience to shape Praxis Initiative's programs, inform policy development, and galvanize stakeholders toward humane, effective reforms within Arizona's criminal legal landscape.

Jessica Johnson

Policy Director & Co-founder
Jessica spent eight years at the Perryville Complex, navigating all custody levels as both inmate and advocate, which forged her profound understanding of institutional shortcomings and reform opportunities. She transforms personal adversity into strategic advocacy, spearheading legislative initiatives and coalition-building efforts designed to improve conditions, expand oversight, and ensure accountability across Arizona's correctional institutions.

Sherri Scates

Operations Coordinator
Sherri witnessed both her sons' incarcerations‚Äîone serving a thirty-year sentence and the other tragically lost to suicide after enduring deplorable conditions‚Äîwhich fueled her unwavering commitment to prison oversight. Drawing on personal tragedy, she courageously champions independent monitoring, legislative reforms, and community engagement to demand transparency, justice, and humane treatment for individuals within Arizona's prison system.

Mindi Kraicinski

Corrections Advisor
Mindi dedicated twenty-six years to ADC service, rising from administrative typist to Correctional Officer III working primarily at the Eyman, Florence and Globe complexes. Her extensive operational expertise spans security protocols, case management, and special management units. She now applies this deep institutional insight to guide Praxis Initiative's accountability measures, training programs, and policy recommendations.

At Praxis Initiative, we understand that the problem isn't just the system itself, but how it operates ‚Äî unaccountable, unchecked, and unfair. We've lived through it, and we know firsthand the need for reform through independent prison oversight.

96% of people in prison are coming home. The question isn't if, but when. We need a system that doesn't leave us worse than when we went in. Our civic engagement programs help prepare returning citizens for successful reintegration.
